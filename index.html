2024 Olympic medals

Determining the most "successful" country in the Olympics is not a trivial matter. The obvious approach that compares total medals fails to account for other significant differences between any two countries. The table below attempts to correct for this discrepancy by calculating the cost, in dollars of GDP, per medal. While not a precise measure, GDP is a reasonable indicator by which the medal counts can be weighted to create a better comparison. In the table below, the last two columns present the:- :-
  1. Cost of each medal as a function of the GDP. A country with a lower GDP will have a lower cost per medal when compared to a country with higher GDP when the medal counts for the two countries is identical.
  2. Cost of each medal as a function of the GDP, weighting for the population size. This table uses the GDP per capita rather than the total GDP to weight the cost per medal. This approach includes both GDP and population difference between two coutries when making medal comparisons.
